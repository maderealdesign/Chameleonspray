Our Kitchen Process

90% of our work is preparation. We follow a strict methodology to ensure your new finish is robust, flawless, and long-lasting.

1

Removal & Prep

We carefully remove all doors, drawer fronts, and plinths, taking them to our workshop. Back at the house, we meticulously mask off your worktops, walls, and appliances.

2

Deep Clean & Key

Every single surface (both workshop items and fixed carcasses) is thoroughly degreased and sanded to create a 'key', ensuring the specialist primer adheres perfectly.

3

Professional Spraying

We apply high-adhesion primers followed by multiple fine coats of your chosen topcoat colour using professional airless/HVLP systems for a flawless finish.

4

Refit & Reveal

Once cured, we return to unmask your kitchen, expertly refit all doors and drawers, install your handles, and leave you with a stunning, transformed space.

Kitchen Respray FAQs

Everything you need to know about our kitchen painting service.

Is the paint durable enough for a kitchen?

Yes. We do not use standard decorators' paint. We use specialist, high-grade coatings specifically designed for cabinetry. Once fully cured, the finish is robust, flexible, wipeable, and highly resistant to the daily wear, tear, and moisture found in a kitchen environment.

How long does the whole process take?

Typically, we will remove your doors and drawers on day one. These are taken to our workshop and usually take 3-5 days to prep and spray. We will then spend 1-2 days at your home carefully masking and spraying the fixed carcasses. Finally, we return to unmask and refit the doors. You can still use your kitchen (carefully) while the doors are away.

Can you spray over peeling laminate?

If the vinyl or laminate wrap is peeling badly, we usually peel it completely off to reveal the MDF core underneath. We then prime and spray the MDF, which actually provides a fantastic, stable surface for our coatings.
